Video games based on Pac-Man are among the most iconic and recognisable titles in gaming history. First appearing in arcades, Pac-Man introduced a unique style of gameplay that focused on movement, pattern recognition, and strategic decision-making rather than shooting or combat. Players guide Pac-Man through maze-like levels, eating pellets while avoiding colourful ghost enemies, each with distinct movement behaviours.

The simple concept hides surprising depth. Power pellets temporarily turn the tables, allowing Pac-Man to chase the ghosts, creating moments of risk-and-reward decision-making. As levels progress, enemy speed increases and patterns become more aggressive, testing player reflexes and memorisation. Bonus items and score-chasing further encourage mastery and replayability.
