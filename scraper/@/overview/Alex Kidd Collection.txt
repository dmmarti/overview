Video games starring **Alex Kidd** represent a classic era of platforming, blending colourful worlds, imaginative challenges, and a strong sense of personality. Created by SEGA, Alex Kidd became one of the company’s earliest mascots, starring in adventures that helped define console gaming during the late 1980s. These games introduced players to a whimsical universe filled with strange creatures, unusual villains, and memorable locations.

Alex Kidd titles typically focus on side-scrolling platform action, requiring precise timing, quick reflexes, and careful exploration. Players guide Alex as he runs, jumps, swims, and uses his signature punches to defeat enemies and break obstacles. Many games add unique mechanics such as vehicles, underwater sections, or puzzle-based challenges, keeping gameplay varied and engaging. One of the most distinctive features of the series is its use of unconventional boss battles, including the famous rock–paper–scissors duels that add an element of surprise and strategy.

Visually, Alex Kidd games are known for their bright colours and charming, cartoon-like style, supported by catchy music that enhances their playful tone. Across different systems, the series evolved while maintaining its core identity, leaving a lasting legacy in the history of platform games.

This collection contains games from Arcades, Computers, Consoles and Handhelds.
