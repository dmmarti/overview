The Super Nintendo Entertainment System (also known as the Super NES, SNES or Super Nintendo) is a 16-bit home video game console developed by Nintendo that was released in 1990 in Japan, 1991 in North America, 1992 in Europe and Australasia (Oceania), and 1993 in South America. <br />In Japan, the system is called the Super Famicom, officially adopting the abbreviated name of its predecessor, the Family Computer, or SFC for short. <br />In South Korea, it is known as the Super Comboy and was distributed by Hyundai Electronics. <br />Although each version is essentially the same, several forms of regional lockout prevent the different versions from being compatible with one another.
