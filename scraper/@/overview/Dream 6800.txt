The Dream was a simple hobby computer with 2K bytes of memory that played game programs on a TV. The name means Domestic Recreational Educational and Adaptive Microcomputer incorporating the Motorola 6800 microprocessor.  The Dream 6800 uses the CHIP-8 programming language.  It was a home based computer kit that was originally released as a series of artices in 1979 in the magazine Electronics Australia and was designed by Michael Bauer.
