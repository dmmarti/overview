	World Cup Carnival became the first FIFA officially licensed title, starting the line that still runs to this day. Initially planned as a US Gold in-house title, time constraints after a failed first attempt forced them to acquire Artic's World Cup Football, and fit the game with licensing tags and 'feelies' including a badge and wallchart, to release the game in time for the competition.    In the Commodore 64 and Amstrad CPC versions, ten teams are available, in the ZX Spectrum the selection is extended to the 24 participating teams.in the real World Cup. In any of the platforms, the competition is limited to eight teams. A training mode with juggling and penalty taking and saving is also included in all versions. There are no formation settings, and matches last for 3 minutes.
